<p><p>The chickpea market is primarily divided into two types: Kabuli and Desi chickpeas. Kabuli chickpeas are larger, lighter-colored, and have a smoother texture, making them popular in Western cuisines and dishes like hummus. Desi chickpeas, smaller and darker, are favored in South Asian cuisines for their robust flavor and are often used in curries and snacks. Both types have distinct market dynamics, with varying demand based on regional preferences and culinary applications, influencing pricing and availability.</p></p>
