<p><ul><li>Organic Extraction Method</li><li>Nonorganic Method</li><li>Adsorption Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The DNA extraction reagent market is categorized into three primary methods: organic extraction, nonorganic extraction, and adsorption. The organic extraction method utilizes solvents like phenol-chloroform to isolate DNA, ensuring high purity but can be hazardous. Nonorganic methods often employ salt solutions for a simpler and safer process, albeit potentially with lower yield. Adsorption methods leverage specific materials, like silica, to bind DNA efficiently, enabling rapid extraction with minimal contaminants. These diverse techniques cater to varying research and clinical needs.</p></p>
